## 1. MAJOR GLOBAL INNOVATION PRIZES

### 1.1 Hult Prize 2026 Impact Fellowship
- **Organization:** Hult Prize Foundation
- **Prize/Funding:** US $1,000,000 in seed funding (Grand Prize)
- **Eligibility:** Open to student and early-stage social enterprise teams globally. Must address UN Sustainable Development Goals.
- **Deadline:** Main competition application deadline was February 2, 2026. Impact Fellowship deadline was December 27, 2025. New cycles expected annually.
- **Application URL:** https://www.hultprize.org/apply-now
- **Why PharmKulen fits:** PharmKulen directly addresses SDG 3 (Good Health and Well-Being) by digitizing pharmacy infrastructure in a developing country, improving medicine availability and healthcare access. The Hult Prize favors for-profit startups solving social problems.

### 1.2 MIT Solve Global Health Challenge
- **Organization:** MIT Solve (Massachusetts Institute of Technology)
- **Prize/Funding:** Selected Solver teams receive prize funding (varies by challenge, typically $10,000-$50,000+), plus support from Johnson & Johnson Foundation as Global Health Anchor Supporter. Access to MIT network and 9-month support program.
- **Eligibility:** Open globally to anyone with a technology-driven solution. Organizations at any stage.
- **Deadline:** 2025 cycle: February 3 - April 17, 2025. 2026 cycle expected to open Q1 2026.
- **Application URL:** https://solve.mit.edu/challenges
- **Why PharmKulen fits:** MIT Solve explicitly seeks solutions that increase capacity and resilience of health systems including supply chains and infrastructure, and increase access to health services for all communities. PharmKulen's pharmacy digitization and medicine supply chain visibility directly match.

### 1.3 Schwab Foundation Social Innovation Awards
- **Organization:** Schwab Foundation / World Economic Forum
- **Prize/Funding:** Recognition, WEF Annual Meeting invitation, global network access. No direct cash prize but massive visibility and connection to funders.
- **Eligibility:** Social innovators globally working in private, public, social, or collective organizations. Nomination-based (400+ nominations received for 2026).
- **Deadline:** Nominations typically open mid-year for January announcement. 2026 winners announced at Davos January 2026.
- **Application URL:** https://www.schwabfound.org/selection-process
- **Why PharmKulen fits:** The Schwab Foundation honors diversity of social innovation models. PharmKulen's tech-driven approach to pharmacy infrastructure in Cambodia represents exactly the kind of systems-level change they celebrate.

### 1.4 Bloomberg New Economy Catalysts

- **Prize/Funding:** Recognition, invitation to Bloomberg New Economy Forum (Singapore), access to global network of decision-makers.

### 1.5 TED Audacious Project

## APPLICATION TIPS FOR PHARMKULEN

1. **Lead with impact metrics**: 710+ pharmacies mapped, 3,993+ medicine entries, 462 images analyzed for one pharmacy alone. These numbers tell a compelling story.

2. **Frame as infrastructure**: Position PharmKulen not just as a startup but as **digital health infrastructure** for developing countries -- this resonates with funders like Gates Foundation, GIF, and MIT Solve.

3. **Emphasize scalability**: The model (pharmacy digitization + unified medicine library + visit mapping) can be replicated in any developing country. This is what GIF, Seedstars, and major impact investors want to hear.

4. **Supply chain angle**: Many grants specifically mention supply chain resilience. PharmKulen creates visibility into the pharmaceutical supply chain that didn't exist before.

5. **SDG alignment**: Always mention SDG 3 (Good Health), SDG 9 (Industry, Innovation, Infrastructure), and SDG 17 (Partnerships). Multiple opportunities require SDG alignment.

6. **Prepare a 2-minute pitch video**: Many applications (Hult Prize, MIT Solve, Web Summit) require video pitches. Create one polished video that can be reused.

7. **Track record**: Document every pharmacy digitized, every medicine catalogued, every visit mapped. Funders want evidence of traction, not just ideas.
